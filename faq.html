Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the QEEPP structural discipline framework and how it is used to evaluate digital transformation integrity.

Digital transformation governance

Why do many digital transformation programs increase complexity instead of capability?

Many modernization programs introduce new technologies faster than operational discipline evolves. Without balance across Quality, Effectiveness, Efficiency, Performance, and Productivity, complexity can grow faster than capability.

How can organizations measure structural integrity in transformation programs?

Structural integrity can be evaluated by examining how consistently operational practices scale across reliability, delivery effectiveness, operational efficiency, system performance, and productivity. QEEPP provides a structured framework for evaluating this balance.

What causes modernization initiatives to fail structurally?

Transformation initiatives often fail structurally when improvements in one operational dimension outpace the others. For example productivity may increase through automation while quality discipline remains weak.

About the QEEPP framework

What is QEEPP?

QEEPP is a structural discipline framework used to evaluate digital transformation integrity across five operational dimensions: Quality, Effectiveness, Efficiency, Performance, and Productivity.

Is QEEPP tied to a specific technology platform?

No. The framework is technology-agnostic and can be applied to cloud migration programs, DevOps transformations, enterprise architecture programs, and platform modernization initiatives.

Using QEEPP assessments

How is a QEEPP assessment performed?

A QEEPP assessment evaluates the five structural dimensions using a scoring scale from 0 to 5. Scores are derived through architectural analysis, operational metrics, or structured interviews.

What does the Starfish diagram represent?

The Starfish diagram visualizes the structural integrity profile of a transformation. Balanced transformations form a proportional star shape, while distortions indicate structural imbalance.

What does the Radar chart show?

The Radar chart compares current structural integrity with a target integrity profile, highlighting where improvements are expected.

What does the Trends matrix show?

The Trends matrix shows structural integrity progression across multiple assessments over time and helps determine whether improvements remain structurally balanced.

Applying QEEPP in organizations

Who typically performs QEEPP assessments?

Assessments are usually performed by enterprise architects, transformation leaders, CTO organizations, or consulting teams responsible for modernization programs.

When should an organization run a QEEPP assessment?

Assessments are most valuable during key transformation stages such as cloud migration planning, architecture restructuring, or operational scaling phases.

How often should assessments be repeated?

Many organizations perform recurring assessments at transformation milestones in order to observe structural integrity trends and ensure structural balance is maintained.

About QEEPP

Who developed the QEEPP framework?

QEEPP was conceived and developed by Rado Dimitrov as a structural discipline framework for guiding complex digital transformation initiatives.

Is the framework publicly available?

Yes. The framework concepts, diagrams, and assessment methodology are documented publicly and can be used as a structural reference for transformation governance.
